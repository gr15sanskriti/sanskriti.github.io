2 Days • In Person • Corporate

Cyber Threat Hunting & Incident Response Workshop

A focused two-day corporate program designed to build proactive detection skills and practical incident response capabilities through theory, demonstrations, quizzes, case studies, CTF, tabletop exercises, and a live-fire exercise in a sandboxed environment.

Deliverables
  • Pre-training reading
  • Participant checklist
  • Lab environment access
  • Exercise datasets
  • After Action Review report
Learning Objectives
  • Understand the difference between threat hunting and traditional security monitoring.
  • Apply a structured threat hunting lifecycle.
  • Use open-source tools for log analysis, forensics, and malware inspection.
  • Execute the IR lifecycle: Preparation, Identification, Containment, Eradication, Recovery, Lessons Learned.
  • Practice decision-making under pressure through tabletop and live-fire scenarios.
  • Produce findings, timelines, remediation recommendations, and AAR outputs.

Day 1 — Threat Hunting Foundations

  1. Introduction: Threat hunting vs traditional security, proactive defense, quiz.
  2. Lifecycle: Hypothesis, investigation, resolution, phishing/lateral movement hypotheses.
  3. Data Sources: Network logs, endpoint logs, SIEM analysis, IoC search.
  4. Tool Demo: ELK / Security Onion style investigation workflow.
  5. Hands-on Exercise: CTF-style hunt for hidden threat, IoCs and TTPs.
  6. Team Presentation: Findings, evidence path, and conclusion.

Day 2 — Incident Response & Crisis Management

  1. IR Process: Six phases of incident response and team responsibilities.
  2. Tabletop Exercise: Ransomware-style scenario with communication and containment decisions.
  3. Forensics: Evidence preservation, memory forensics, malware triage.
  4. Tools: Volatility / PEStudio style guided practical lab.
  5. Live Fire: Sandboxed incident with hunting, containment, eradication, and recovery.
  6. AAR: What went well, gaps, recommendations, and next-step roadmap.

Cyber for AI Workshop

A two-day deep-dive program helping security teams understand, detect, and respond to emerging AI-driven cyber threats through an immersive breach storyline involving a compromised AI chatbot.

Scenario

An AI-powered chatbot starts leaking SQL snippets and sensitive financial data. The SOC investigates prompt manipulation, backend credential exposure, persistence, and lateral movement.

Day 1 — Detecting the Breach

  1. AI Threat Landscape: Risks, benefits, real-world AI breach case studies.
  2. AI-Specific Threats: Adversarial ML, chatbot exploitation, AI misuse examples.
  3. OWASP Top 10 for LLMs: Prompt injection, sensitive disclosure, insecure outputs, and related risks.
  4. Hands-on Range Lab: Detect and mitigate prompt injection to prevent data leakage.
  5. SOC Review: Evidence, IOCs, exploited vulnerability, knowledge quiz, Q&A.

Day 2 — Responding to the Attack

  1. Applying AI RMF: Risk identification, governance, and AI breach response planning.
  2. Case Workshop: Apply AI RMF to the chatbot breach.
  3. Bonus: Implementing self-hosted LLM using open-source tools.
  4. MITRE Mapping: Use ATT&CK + ATLAS to understand adversary behavior.
  5. Defensive Controls: LLM request filters, guardrails, anomaly detection, SIEM integrations.
  6. Wrap-Up: Final review, feedback, lessons learned, and incident closure.

Strata CyberRange Platform

Strata CyberRange is a professional cyber training and exercise platform designed for realistic, automated, and measurable defensive practice. It is not a traditional static CTF. It functions like a self-refereeing cyber exercise environment where scenarios are provisioned, attacks are emulated, detections are generated, and participant progress is scored automatically.

How the CyberRange Works

  • Scenario Provisioning: Isolated virtual environments are created for each exercise with required systems, configurations, vulnerabilities, and monitoring components.
  • Attack Emulation: Realistic adversary behavior is triggered based on the scenario objective, allowing participants to detect and investigate meaningful activity.
  • Detection & Evidence: Logs, events, alerts, and security telemetry are generated during the exercise to support SOC-style investigation.
  • Automated Task Validation: Participant actions are mapped to scenario objectives so completed tasks can be detected and scored without manual grading.
  • Scoreboard & AAR: Progress, points, gaps, and lessons learned are consolidated into a structured review for training improvement.
